# Research Design Standards Alignment

## Status

This protocol defines standards-aligned disclosure expectations for Synthetix study plans and reports. It is not a certification claim.

## References

- ISO 20252-style market, opinion, social research, insights, and data analytics service requirements.
- AAPOR disclosure expectations for public opinion and survey research reports.
- ICC/ESOMAR ethics and transparency principles for market, opinion, social research, and data analytics.

## Required Disclosure Checklist

Every professional study plan and report must cover:

- Research purpose and client/sponsor context, or an explicit statement that no sponsor is declared.
- Population under study and unit of analysis.
- Synthetic sampling or simulation frame, including inclusion/exclusion rules.
- Questionnaire/instrument wording and response options.
- Measurement context, instructions, and any stimuli.
- Segment variables and planned subgroup cuts.
- Weighting method, or an explicit no-weighting statement.
- Data processing and validation rules.
- Qualitative coding method and whether coding is deterministic, model-assisted, human-coded, or mixed.
- Sample size and denominator disclosure for every reported percentage or distribution.
- Attrition, refusals, retries, exclusions, and suppression rules.
- Limitations and non-inferential-use warning.
- Model, provider, prompt, protocol, seed, and manifest provenance.
- Appendix detail sufficient for independent review of synthetic-method claims.

## Forbidden Claims

- Do not claim representative human research.
- Do not claim population prevalence, statistical significance, confidence intervals, causality, or replacement of human surveys.
- Do not claim ISO, AAPOR, ICC, ESOMAR, or WAPOR certification.
- Do not describe selected benchmark metric pass rates as full paper, full report, full table, full graph, or wording equivalence.

## Required Wording

Use `standards-aligned disclosure checklist` for this layer.

Use `selected metric pass rate` for benchmark scoring.

Use `synthetic scenario evidence` or `preliminary simulation evidence` for results.
