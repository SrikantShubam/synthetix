# 12 Research Basis and Professional Report Recovery

## Problem

The current golden-path proof can pass while still failing the actual product goal. It checks that the system emits enough headings, tables, warnings, chart files, and denominators, but it does not prove that Synthetix follows the research basis behind digital-persona survey approximation, nor that generated reports meet professional survey-report expectations.

## Priority Order

1. P1: Follow the actual digital-persona survey research basis.
2. P2: Follow Synthetix's own stated product basis and fixture contracts.
3. P3: Meet standards-aligned professional report disclosure and depth gates.
4. P4: Preserve reference-paper comparison and expand chart decision quality.

## Requirements

- The golden paper contract must be operational, not decorative. Professional proof cannot pass unless the run states how it aligns with the public research basis: background variables, prior/source context, held-out or dry-run task boundary, distributional evaluation, equity/segment checks, clustering or multivariate-structure limitation, retrieval/context limits, and human-validation handoff.
- The professional fixture must not approve toy panel sizes such as 10 or 12 as professional-quality proof when the source report has hundreds of respondents. Tiny panels can run only as lightweight or novice dry runs, or as professional runs that fail quality gates.
- Target population size, source sample size, and synthetic panel size must remain separate in every intake/report artifact.
- Professional report quality must require survey disclosure adequacy, not just section presence. The gate must check population, source/sample, synthetic panel, questionnaire wording/options, base-size policy, nonresponse/weighting disclosure, analysis plan, qualitative coding plan, limitations, provenance, and human-fieldwork handoff.
- Chart decisions must be auditable. A professional chart decision must include status, reason, and planned visual type or replacement type. Rendered visual types must not default to bars by accident, and suppressed/replaced visuals must state the cause.
- Report and chart improvements must not use holdout/source-of-truth data, benchmark answer keys, or fixture answer-bearing fields.

## Non-Goals

- Do not claim certification by ISO, AAPOR, ICC, ESOMAR, or WAPOR.
- Do not claim synthetic outputs are representative human survey results.
- Do not imply selected metric pass rate equals full paper, table, chart, wording, qualitative-code, or report replication.
- Do not add vector databases, distributed workers, or new storage infrastructure for this quality fix.

## Acceptance

- Unit tests fail professional quality when the research-basis alignment fields are absent.
- Unit tests fail professional quality when synthetic panel size is below the professional minimum and the run is not explicitly lightweight.
- Golden-path fixture validation fails a professional survey dry run with a toy synthetic panel.
- Golden-path deterministic proof can generate more than 12 professional respondents without index errors or hard-coded answer arrays.
- Report quality fails when standards-aligned disclosure fields are shallow or generic.
- Chart decision contracts expose visual type or replacement type and reject rendered chart decisions without an auditable visual decision.
- Golden-path review records research-basis, fixture-scale, report-disclosure, and chart-contract failures separately.
